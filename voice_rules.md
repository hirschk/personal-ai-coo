# Voice Rules — Sterl Learning Loop
Last updated: 2026-04-21

## Outreach Rules
- No filler openers like "Hope you're well" — get straight to the point
- Short sentences
- No em dashes
- Explicit ask at the end
- "Thanks either way" is a good closer
- No corporate buzzwords

## LinkedIn Post Rules
- Short sentences
- No em dashes
- No dramatic openers
- No "excited to share"
- PM-native language: "shipped", "mapped to outcomes", "found in the data"
- Slightly understated — tighten, don't add colour
- Standalone punchy lines on their own paragraph
- Ends with a real question the audience would actually answer
- Under 200 words
- First person

## LinkedIn Post Footer (always append)

```
Open to my next role. Head of Product or Senior PM at fintechs in NYC, Toronto, LA, Miami, Austin, or remote. Hit me if AI is core to what you're building.
```

## Learned Rules — LinkedIn Posts (from edits — Apr 21)
- Open with an audience qualifier: "job hunting?" or similar to target who the post is for before you make the observation
- Don't explain your motivation — "Not because I thought it would close a job offer..." is over-justifying. Let the action speak.
- Cut the moralizing paragraph: no neat packaging of the lesson ("the gap between 'interested' and 'proof'") — too preachy
- Use "Show > tell" as a compressed standalone line instead of a paragraph explaining the takeaway
- "I'm interested in" not "I respect" — more honest, less flattery
- Use colon to introduce inline lists: "working AI agent: reconciliation, month-end close" not em dash
- "this time I built and sent something relevant instead" — grounded and personal beats punchy and clever
- Don't coin aphorisms ("The demo is the message") — too polished. Just say the thing and stop.
- Lowercase continuation lines — "this time" not "This time"

## Learned Rules — LinkedIn Posts (from edits — Apr 15)
- Don't write clever one-liners to sound smart ("That's the unlock.") — just state the thing
- Use precise industry language, not tech-person approximations ("couldn't stand up to audit" not "broke on a different PDF format")
- Show don't summarize — a quoted example beats a paragraph describing what agents can do
- Use lists for things that are actually lists, not prose
- CTAs should invite the audience to answer from their own experience, not be rhetorical either/or closers
- Business case is not optional — hours saved per FTE and outcomes make people share; workflow alone doesn't
- No em dashes anywhere — in drafts, outreach, posts. Hard rule. Looks like AI wrote it.
- Open with lowercase "hey" for casual outreach — not "Hey"
- Break messages into short standalone lines, not flowing paragraphs
- "I'm interviewing" not "I'm currently interviewing" — cut every filler word
- Use parentheticals for context: "(ledger automation product)" not a full clause
- Never open with "Hope you're enjoying your time at X" or any variation — banned
- 3-day follow-up timing on all outreach, not 7

## Learned Rules — Recruiter Outreach (from edits — Apr 22)
- Open with "hi (name)" — lowercase, no comma, no filler after
- Lead with what you want, not who you are
- Include stage qualifier: "Series B+ fintech" not just "fintech"
- Include company context inline: "Hearth (NYC, Series B)" — gives recruiter instant signal
- "Senior AI PM" not "Senior PM" — the AI qualifier matters for fintech roles
- Close with "If you have a relevant role, I'd love to hear about it" — direct, not vague (not "if anything's moving")
- Keep to 3 short paragraphs max

## Learned Rules — Emails (from edits — Apr 16)
- Forwardable intro emails have TWO parts: (1) outer wrapper to the referrer, (2) inner forwardable blurb
- Outer wrapper: casual, personal, reference something specific about them ("Nice seeing Zero Collective growing"), low-pressure ask ("If you're happy with it, feel free to fwd below")
- Inner forwardable opens with a punchy one-liner: "Senior PM for you:" — not flowery or warm
- Add "at scale" to the positioning: "building AI and fintech products from 0 to 1 and at scale"
- Referrer sign-off is just "Happy chatting -- [Name]" — not "I'll leave it with you two" or any elaborate close
- Subject to the referrer: "Intro request Hirsch <> [Target]" — not "Fwd intro"
- Keep the outer wrapper to 2-3 sentences max. Don't over-explain the ask.
- Reference the specific contact/team they were looking for in the nudge: "Any luck finding that Payments team talent acq contact?" not just "Any luck finding that contact?"
- Subject line format for follow-up forwardables: "Intro Hirsch ([Title]) <> [Company] [Team]"
- Use the actual relationship origin in the inner blurb: "Hirsch and I met at startup events in London" — not a generic "we go way back"
- Title in forwardables: "Lead AI PM" not "Senior fintech PM"
- Forwardable intro emails are written FROM the referrer's voice, not Hirsch's — Vino sends it, not Hirsch
- Lead with the referrer's relationship to Hirsch ("We worked together at...") — that's the credibility
- Use bullet points with hard numbers: ARR, cost savings, timeframes, deal size — no vague claims
- Bullet format: outcome/metric first, then context ("Deloitte: audit automation to $13M ARR across 20+ markets")
- Include a one-line positioning statement ("He builds new product lines at established fintechs")
- End casually: "I'll leave it with you guys" — no pressure, no ask
- Never write "I'm in active job search mode" in a forwardable — that's Hirsch talking; let the referrer do the selling
- Sign-off is still "Thanks, Hirsch" at the bottom so Michael knows who to reply to
- Subject: "Fwd intro [First] <> [First]" — not a formal subject line

## Learned Rules — LinkedIn Posts (from edits — May 1)
- Kill the fear first — if the incident sounds bad, address the obvious concern ("No PII exposed") before readers stop reading
- Ground the tool in one line upfront: "Personal bot built on OpenClaw, running on Telegram" — context before story
- Add levity with translation/emoji for foreign words: "(translation → 'Sherlock' 😂)" — humanizes, lowers alarm
- Prose over bullets for personal incident narratives — lists feel clinical; sentences feel lived-in
- Reframe failure as fast recovery: "fixed in 5 minutes" beats "two hours of incident response" — downplay the pain, show the agency
- Name the PM learning angle explicitly: "I'm a PM learning to build with AI" — this IS the hook for his audience; don't bury
- "Battle scar" framing: reframe failure as a feature of personal projects, not a cautionary tale — "exactly the kind of battle scar I want" lands better than incident drama
- Warm community close beats question CTA on personal/learning posts: "Have fun building, but with care" — doesn't demand engagement, feels generous not transactional
- Drop the job search footer on posts that have a strong personal/community angle — it breaks the tone
- Open with a specific reference to recent context ("Good chatting with you Friday") not a generic "taking you up on"
- Forwardable blurb format: "[Title] for you: [Name], most recently at [Company] ([specific descriptor e.g. Series B BNPL Fintech]) [active work e.g. shipping agentic AI]. Exploring [roles/location]. Could I connect you two?"
- Use specific fintech descriptor, not just "Series B fintech" (e.g. "BNPL Fintech", "neobank", "payments")
- "shipping agentic AI" beats passive positioning — show what they were doing
- Include location in the forwardable blurb when relevant ("Exploring Product roles in Toronto")
- End the forwardable with "Could I connect you two?" so the referrer can paste and send
- Send a separate message per company when asking for multiple intros

## Learned Rules — Emails (from edits — Apr 8)
- Lead the subject line with the role/topic context, not just "Following up"
- "Thanks for the conversation today" not "Thank you for the time today"
- "most recently in finding..." — understated, not "unlocking" (less hype)
- Drop the question about their company — they know what they build
- "feels like a natural home for that kind of work" > "maps well to what you're building"
- For CPO/senior interviews: reference the specific thing they said that stuck with you — shows you were listening
- If you shared something notable (GitHub, demo), include the link in the follow-up
- Sign off with first name only ("Tim" not "Timothy") if that's how you spoke
- Keep it under 100 words
